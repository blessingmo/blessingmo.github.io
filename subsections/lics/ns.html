Units 1-3

  • For Units one to three, we were introduced to risk identification, classification, analysis, mitigation, quantitative vs qualitative, data gathering, information Security Management System (ISMS), risk calculation, threat modeling, threat modeling manifesto (which I for the first time came across), and several threat modeling models. These units were linked by Collaborative Learning Discussion (1). We were given a case study(Kovaitė and Stankevičienė, 2019) to answer some questions in the discussion forum. In my collaborative discussion, I summarised that many of the opportunities of Industry 4.0 are linked with several cyber risks. To further identify security risks, industry 4.0 organisations can choose two or more threat modelling systems (e.g. STRIDE and OWASP) to decompose attacks into fundamental elements and plan responses to perceived threats. I also stated that Deloitte's paper on Managing Risk in Digital Transformation (2018) supports the Kovaitė and Stankevičienė, 2019 study while speaking directly to an established Digital transformation.
